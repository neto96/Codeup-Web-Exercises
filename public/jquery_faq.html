National Parks FAQ

Total land that is a National Park:
Over 84 million acres!
First national park:
Yellowstone, created in 1872 by President Ulysses S. Grant.
First cave named a national park in the world:
Wind Cave. It also happens to be the sixth longest cave in the world.
Smallest national park:
Hot Springs National Park - 5,500 acres.
Biggest national park:
Wrangell - St. Elias National Park - 8.3 million acres.
Only state without a national park:
Delaware. Sorry Delaware!
Lowest elevation in the US:
Death Valley National Park - 282 feet below sea level.
Deepest cave in the nation:
Carlsbad Caverns National Park - 1,593 feet deep.
Second national park:
Mackinac National Park - no longer a national park, but a state park.
Most visited national park:
Great Smoky Mountains National Park - more than 10 million recreational visits each year.

Big Bend National Park

  • Only state park to have an entire mountain range within its borders.
  • Named after the "big bend" in the Rio Grande.
  • At least 450 species of birds inhabit the park.
  • Established in June 12, 1944.

Yellowstone National Park

  • World's oldest national park.
  • Established in 1872.
  • Wyoming, Idaho, and Montana were not states yet when it was established.
  • It was administered by the U.S. Army until 1916, when the NPS was established.

Hot Springs National Park

  • Smallest national park.
  • Calls itself the "oldest area in the national park system".
  • There are over 150 bird species within the park.
  • Established in March 4, 1921.
